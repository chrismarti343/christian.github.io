Description of target audience

The target audience in this web site is designed for traveler people and families every day. Have you ever happen that you are planning a trip to a specific place, and need quick information about the weather of my destination? So, most of the time including me. I would go to a weather forecast web site to be prepared or to change our trip travel day, or only to be prepared for the day.

This web page will be helpful for young couple travelers, families, and groups of friends. They are more likely to look on the internet for the forecast before travel and before leaving the house.

Two personas

  • First person : BYUI students | Jhon Stones

    They will be using this web page because students are more likely to go on a round trip with their friends.

    Students also are more likely to use their cellphone and browse from the cellphone. Since the app is responsive it is going to be because way easier to pull up their phones and lookup for this theclimatetoday.com

    They are around 18 - 30 years old. Their education level is obviously college students. Since they are looking for getting fast and efficient information on the web sites, this web site will be very helpful for them.

  • Second person : Families | Jonathan Williams

    They will be using this web page because many families plan a trip for vacations, also many families, especially the head of the family is the one in charge of taking care of the other family members. So that implies they will be always prepared to support their children whether if they go on a vacation and the weather is very bad to they go out.

    This group of people is from 5 years old to 45 years old. This group of people is a little bit wide. However, the head of the family is the most likely people to use this web site.

    They will use this web site more than the students because the family depends every day on this information. On weekdays the head of the family can take a quick glance at the web page, and on the weekend since many families plan their next week on Saturdays or Sundays so they will use the web site a little longer than on weekdays.

Five scenarios

  • First: Carlos and his future wife.

    Carlos has planned a round trip. He has been planning to go to a park and have a picnic with his girlfriend. To do that, he makes sure the day they would go to the park will be sunny. Carlos takes a look at the theclimatetoday.com some days before this activity.

  • Second: Round trip to Arches National Park .

    Carlos has planned a round trip from Rexburg to Utah. They want to visit the Arches National Park for a couple of hours and then come back to Rexburg. Since it is very risky driving in the snow they will need to know the weather forecast before departure from Rexburg.

  • Third: Jhon a his family plan summer break trip .

    In this case. Jhon starts to plan his travel to Cancun. Once he already buys the travel tickets, he needs to know and make sure that the clothes they are going to carry are according to the weather when they get there over there. Jhon uses the web site to see. Turn out that half of the days he will be staying in Cancun are raining, so he prepares some proper clothing.

  • Fourth: Chrsitian a his friends go hiking .

    Christian and his friends from FHE have planned activity to go hiking R mountain. In order to pick them perfect day to hike, they go to theclimatoday.com web site to pick up a sunny day so they can make the most of the hiking.

  • Fifth. Simon's propose marriege

    Simon and Amanda have dated for a couple of months, and they talk to each other about getting married. Since it is winter. Simon comes up with a proposal marriage idea. He will take Amanda snowboarding and propose to her right before leaving that place. To make it even better, he wants to know what time is best to do that, and to snowboarding, so they could have cool photos. So, Simon go to theclimatoday.com to pick up the hour most likely to snow and have this awesome experience.
